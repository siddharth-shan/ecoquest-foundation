Flagship Program
Educational Game

EcoQuest: Guardians of the Green

Our flagship educational game designed to teach players about ecosystems, pollution, and conservation through interactive storytelling and challenges.

About the Game

Guardians of the Green is an immersive adventure game where players take on the role of environmental guardians tasked with protecting various ecosystems from pollution and degradation. Through engaging storylines and science-based challenges, students learn about:

  • Ecosystem dynamics and biodiversity
  • Pollution sources and mitigation strategies
  • Climate change impacts and solutions
  • Renewable energy and sustainability
  • Wildlife conservation and habitat protection

Monthly Missions

EcoChallenge

Monthly interactive missions that engage students in real-world environmental actions, from reducing plastic use to creating habitat gardens.

How It Works

Each month, we release a new EcoChallenge that focuses on a specific environmental issue or sustainable practice. Students complete the challenge and share their experiences, creating a community of young environmental advocates.

Recent Challenges

November 2024

Zero-Waste Week

Reduce personal waste to near-zero for one week and track what you learn about consumption habits.

October 2024

Native Plant Garden

Create a small native plant garden or container garden that supports local pollinators.

September 2024

Plastic-Free Lunch

Pack waste-free lunches for a month and calculate plastic waste prevented.

August 2024

Energy Audit

Conduct a home energy audit and implement three energy-saving changes.

Challenge Benefits

  • Hands-on application of environmental concepts
  • Monthly variety keeps engagement high
  • Shareable achievements on social media
  • Community recognition and badges
  • Real-world impact measurement

Hands-On Conservation

Community Cleanup & Conservation Events

Partner with us for beach and park cleanups across California, working alongside Scouting of America troops and local organizations.

What We Do

Our community events transform digital learning into tangible environmental action. We organize and lead conservation activities that empower young people to make a real difference in their local ecosystems.

Event Types

  • Beach Cleanups: Remove plastic and debris from coastal areas along California beaches
  • Park Restoration: Native plant installation, invasive species removal, and trail maintenance
  • Waterway Protection: Stream cleanups and water quality monitoring
  • Educational Workshops: On-site learning about local ecology and conservation
  • Community Science: Citizen science projects tracking local biodiversity

Partner Organizations

We collaborate with:

  • Scouting of America troops
  • Local environmental nonprofits
  • Schools and youth groups
  • Community organizations
  • Parks departments
